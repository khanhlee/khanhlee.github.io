Protein function prediction is one of the well-studied topics, attracting attentions from countless researchers in computational biology field. Implementing deep neural networks that help improve the prediction of protein function, however, still presents a major challenge. This talk suggests a new strategy that includes gated recurrent units and position-specific scoring matrix profiles to predict some protein functions of great importance. This combination architecture shows superiority in results on the same datasets against all other state-of-the-art algorithms. Through the suggested research, I had given a technique for the discovery of more proteins, particularly proteins connected with specific functions. In addition, our accomplishment could encourage the use of recurrent networks and gated recurrent units in bioinformatics in general and protein function prediction in particular.
